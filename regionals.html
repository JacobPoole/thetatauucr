Keynote Speaker
INSPIRE INTEGRITY: CHASING AN AUTHENTIC LIFE - Corey's talk encourages audiences to chase the truly important things in life such as a solid character, strong personal relationships and a sense of contentment. These “real rabbits” are compared and contrasted to worldly success such as excessive wealth, popularity and prestige. Audience members leave with a better sense of what it takes to develop character, set priorities, gain a big-picture perspective and chase an authentic life.

COREY'S BIO - An Associate Professor of Business Ethics and Legal Studies at the University of Denver, Corey Ciocchetti is one of the University’s most popular and highest-rated professors. Corey joined DU after graduating with a law degree from Duke University School of Law, a Masters degree in Religious Studies and two Bachelors degrees in Finance and Economics--summa cum laude—from the University of Denver.
Corey is a talented speaker and teacher and has won multiple teaching and speaking awards including the Outstanding Professor of the Year Award by the University of Denver Alumni Association and the Joel Goldman Award for most respected speaker on the CAMPUSPEAK roster. He currently teaches classes on business law and ethics in a department ranked by the Wall Street Journal and Business Week in the top ten nationwide for producing students with high ethical standards.

Breakout Sessions

The Physics of Superheroes w/ Dr. Suveen Mathaudhu
         Dr. Mathaudhu's research research focuses on the underpinning mechanisms that will make metallic materials and composites lighter and stronger. He is also an expert on the science of superheroes as depicted in comic books and their associated movies, and frequently speaks to the media and consults on this subject. For example, the fictional element vibranium, which is a component of the indestructible alloy used in Captain America’s shield, is used to introduce real-world alloys that could lower the weight of cars and improve fuel efficiency. Dr. Mathaudhu will be speaking at the first two breakout sessions in Bourns A265.

"Building Blocks" for STEM Research and Education w/ Dr. Will Grover        
          Dr. Grover will share his latest work on developing a set of LEGO-like “building blocks” for creating instruments.  Using these blocks, researchers with no engineering expertise can construct custom instruments for their own unique needs quickly and inexpensively.  Additionally, high school students can use these blocks to perform real engineering practices in their biology and chemistry classes. Dr. Grover will be speaking at the second two breakout sessions in Bourns A265.
​Entrepreneurship and Discovering Your Passion w/ Jeffrey McDaniel
       Entrepreneurship is a mindset, not an occupation. An entrepreneur is someone who is able to identify opportunity, and take advantage of that opportunity to better themselves or those around them. The skills and abilities that it takes to be a successful 'entrepreneur' in the business world can be just as easily applied to student orgs, within larger companies, in charitable organizations, and even in your own personal life! CHUNG 205/206
  ​
Alumni Talk: Finding a Job after Graduation 
          Chapter alumni Vinci Sevilla Jr. (EE 2013) and Memphis Huff V (ME 2015) will discuss their experience and expertise in job hunting after graduation and describe life as an alum. INTN 1002

​Member Involvement/Open Discussion 
          Our current E-board will host this open discussion. Anyone is welcome to drop by at any time. The purpose of this session is for people to discuss the problems their chapter is facing and get feedback from other schools in the Western Region. INTN 1006.
